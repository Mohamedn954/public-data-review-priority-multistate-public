## Resolved discrepancy: Washington phone-clustering 53 vs. 50 (2026-07-16)

`scripts/build_linkage_robustness.py`'s first draft reconstructed phone keys independently from raw `Phone` strings and got 53 facilities at threshold≥3, versus the published baseline of 50. Root-caused via a full facility-level membership comparison (private repo: `PRIVATE-public-data-review-priority-afh/private/WA_53_vs_50_membership_comparison_PRIVATE.md`): exactly three Washington facilities share one phone number that the upstream data construction had deliberately excluded from its `phone_key` field (left blank) as a likely generic/non-facility-specific contact number. The independent reconstruction had no way to know about that exclusion. **Resolution**: the script's primary phone construction (`phone_key_upstream (PRIMARY)`) now reads the raw file's own upstream `phone_key` column directly (exactly reproducing the published 50), with the from-scratch digits-only reconstruction retained and clearly labeled `phone_digits_only_no_upstream_exclusion (SENSITIVITY)`, not a competing baseline. This is explained in three places, consistently: the script's own docstring and inline comments, this provenance entry, and the manuscript (§3.5) and Appendix G (portability discussion). No manuscript or appendix number should ever have used 53 as a primary figure.

## Public vs. privacy-constrained reproducibility, summarized

**Fully reproducible from the public repository alone**: `verify_multistate_numbers.py`'s existing checks (extended, see below); `build_combined_matrix.py`; `build_figure1.py`, `build_figure2.py`, `build_figure3.py`; `build_sensitivity_analysis.py`; `build_observability_analysis.py`; `build_cluster_enforcement_exploratory.py`; the anonymized-data half of `build_corroboration_analysis.py`.

**Figure-hash caveat**: the three figure scripts reproduce *pixel-identical* visual content under any `requirements.txt`-compatible matplotlib version (>=3.7,<4.0) — verified directly under 3.9.4 and 3.10.8, both matching the committed PNGs' rendered content exactly. Their SHA-256 hashes are not guaranteed to match across matplotlib versions, however, because matplotlib embeds its own version string in each PNG's metadata (`tEXt` chunk). `checksums.sha256` confirms the *released* figure files are unmodified from what was distributed; it is not a claim that any compatible environment will reproduce byte-identical figure files from source. Data and script outputs (every CSV, every check in `verify_multistate_numbers.py`) do not have this issue and are exact-byte reproducible from any compatible environment.

**Aggregate-verifiable but dependent on privacy-sensitive source processing that cannot itself be rerun from the public package**: `build_linkage_robustness.py` (reads raw, non-anonymized phone/address strings from sibling private repos); the Oregon business-registry lookups underlying `A5_OR_Cluster_Corroboration_ANON.csv` (raw facility names/registry records are in the private Oregon repo only). Both are documented with full processing code and normalization/search rules, plus verified aggregate outputs, but the identifier-level computation itself requires unreleased raw data a public clone does not have.

**Not part of the public package, by design**: `derivation_audit/` (this repo's internal research log — MN/federal source screening sessions, search protocol, corpus adjudication working files); `PRIVATE-public-data-review-priority-afh/private/` and `PRIVATE-public-data-review-priority-oregon/private/` (raw facility identifiers and the WA 53-vs-50 diagnostic). These materials support the audit trail described in Appendices G/H but are not required to reproduce any number in the released de-identified package, and contain either PII or process detail beyond the scope of the replication package. Nothing PII-bearing exists in `derivation_audit/` itself (verified by pattern search 2026-07-16), but it remains a private research log, not a public release artifact, to keep the public package's scope matched to what's actually needed for reproduction.

## What `scripts/verify_multistate_numbers.py` checks

Every facility-level count in the table above marked "Yes" is independently recomputed by this script directly from `anonymized_data/`, and the script fails loudly (non-zero exit) on any mismatch. See "Verification script extension" in `README.md` for the full current list of checks, extended for this manuscript revision to cover the sensitivity, corroboration, and observability outputs alongside the original headline counts. It does not and cannot verify the federal-audit or congressional-letter facts, since those are not facility-level data; those are documented with source URLs and access dates in `SOURCE_MANIFEST.md` instead.
