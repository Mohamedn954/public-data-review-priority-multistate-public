## 2026-07-17 — AI classification-consistency check (A6) and resulting correction

A single-author-coding limitation was raised: Section 3.1's derivation audit is the sole author re-auditing their own earlier coding, which is rigorous in intent but is not inter-rater reliability. Rather than mislabel a second AI pass as a "second human coder" or claim genuine IRR from it (both explicitly rejected, since this would misrepresent the methodology), a context-blind AI agent instance was used as a disclosed supplementary classification-consistency check — reported as exactly what it is, never as inter-rater reliability.

**Method**: the three MN source documents (2009 OLA, 2017 OLA, DOJ 2026 press release) were freshly downloaded and extracted; a blinded list of the 21 active final constructs (plus retired RF-02) was built with no anchor-status information attached; a fresh, context-blind general-purpose agent — no access to this project's prior conclusions, Table G1, or the author's classifications — independently classified all 22 rows against a written five-category rubric, grounding every non-"Not derivable" classification in a quoted passage.

**Result**: 11 of 22 exact-category agreement (50.0%), reported without a chance-corrected statistic (kappa is not stable at n=22 against a single non-blinded rater) and without softening. Full comparison in `derivation_audit/A6_AI_Classification_Consistency_Check.md`; the fresh agent's complete unedited output in `A6_Fresh_Agent_Raw_Output.md`.

**Verified finding, not just disagreement**: two of the eleven disagreements (RF-14B, RF-17B) involved passages the original A1 keyword search had missed entirely, not differing interpretations of the same passage.
